How to Participate

  • 29 August 2016

Each participating team must submit an on-line abstract for the EWRI 2017 congress (www.ewricongress.org) that discusses briefly the proposed approach (e.g., machine learning techniques, Kalman filer-based approaches, CUSUM-based methodologies etc.). When submitting the abstract, the topic area must be identified as "BATtle of the Attack Detection ALgorithms (BATADAL)" - this will identify your team of authors as a participant in the competition. Once an abstract has been submitted to BATADAL as described above, forward a copy of the abstract to Riccardo Taormina, Stefano Galelli and Nils Tippenhauer (see contact data). A package of problem data and network model will be forwarded to all authors submitting abstracts that describe an attack detection approach. Authors are asked not to forward the problem data and network model to non-participants, so that the organizers can maintain a reliable participant list. Each team must summarize their final design results in a brief congress paper, which must be uploaded to the same web site no later than January 9, 2017 (see schedule). All conforming designs will be included in the public presentation of summary results at the congress and will be published as part of the congress proceedings. Submitted papers describing the final designs from each team should be brief and to the point. It is not necessary to describe the competition, as that will be included in the summary comparison paper. Each paper should briefly state that it is part of the BATtle of the Attack Detection ALgorithms (BATADAL) competition. To allow efficient and fair assessment of contributed algorithms, papers submitted to BATADAL are asked to include the following sections: Abstract; Introduction (brief); Methodology; Summary of results for the first two datasets ; Conclusions; and References. The submission of the detection report to the organizers is compulsory - reports submitted with incomplete information may be excluded from the comparison. Participants have the option to submit a revised paper to EWRI congress to include the results on the test dataset.
